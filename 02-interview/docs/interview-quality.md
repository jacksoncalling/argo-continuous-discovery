# Interview Quality — Teresa Torres' Criteria

## The Core Distinction: Story vs. Opinion

Torres requires **story-based interviews** that collect "specific stories about past
real-world behavior." The unit of reliable data is a **specific experience** — one
event, one time, with concrete details.

When interviews collect opinions or predictions instead, the data is lower fidelity.
People's stated preferences diverge from their actual behavior. This is not a moral
judgment — it is a measurement constraint.

## Why Specific Stories Matter

When asked an abstract question ("How do you usually buy gifts?"), the interviewee
constructs a **composite narrative** — blending details from multiple occasions into
one smooth answer. This composite:

- Averages out the edge cases (which are often the most revealing)
- Omits friction points they've normalized
- Reflects how they *think* they behave, not how they *actually* behave
- Gets more polished (and less truthful) the more times they tell it

When asked about a specific occasion ("Tell me about the last time you bought a gift
on our website — what happened?"), the interviewee recalls **one real event** with:

- Concrete sequence (first I did X, then Y happened)
- Specific friction (I got stuck at Z)
- Actual behavior (I ended up doing W)
- Emotional context (I felt frustrated because...)

The specific story is the raw material. The composite is already processed.

## Question Patterns to Look For

### Story-inviting (high value)
- "Tell me about the last time you..."
- "Walk me through what happened when..."
- "What did you do next?"
- "Can you think of a specific time when...?"
- "What was the most recent occasion where...?"

### Generalized-inviting (moderate value)
- "How do you usually...?"
- "What's your typical process for...?"
- "What do you normally do when...?"

### Opinion-inviting (low value for opportunity extraction)
- "What do you think about...?"
- "Would you use X if we built it?"
- "How do you feel about...?"
- "What would you want us to change?"
- "What features are you missing?"

## Source Quality Beyond Interviews

Torres notes that opportunities from **sales conversations, support tickets, and feature
requests** are "missing context" — they tell you WHAT was requested but not WHY, or in
what specific situation. These sources can inspire interview questions but should not be
treated as equivalent to story-based interview data.

| Source | Use It For | Do NOT Use It For |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| Story-based interview | Extracting opportunities with full confidence range | — |
| Support ticket | Generating interview questions ("Ask about X in next interview") | Direct opportunity extraction |
| Feature request | Noting as a solution signal, investigating the need behind it | Treating as a validated opportunity |
| Sales conversation | Understanding what customers ask about | Building the tree |
| NPS / survey comment | Flagging themes to explore in interviews | Classifying as Strong confidence |

## The 3-4 Interview Minimum

Torres: "Conduct at least 3 story-based customer interviews" before starting to map the
opportunity space. The tree should start forming after 3-4 interviews and stabilize in
structure (though not in content) after 6-8.

Processing fewer than 3 interviews is allowed — the opportunities are just provisional.
Mark them as such.
