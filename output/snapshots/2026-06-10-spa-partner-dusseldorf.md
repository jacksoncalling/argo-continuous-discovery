### Escalation Detail — Opportunity 1

**Trigger:** Channel conflict / business model tension

**What happened:** Partner invested 45 minutes in expert skin analysis using Babor's own tool (Derma Visualizer). Client left without buying. Three days later, client purchased the recommended products from babor.com using a 10% loyalty voucher. Partner lost 30% retail commission.

**Why this escalates:** The team's product outcome (increase spa-to-online purchases) directly undermines the partner channel's revenue model. Achieving the outcome without addressing the commission structure risks partner defection to competitor brands. Partner named Janssen Cosmetics and Dr. Grandel as alternatives.

**Who should review:** Product leadership + channel/partner management

**Suggested constraint on outcome:** "Increase spa-to-online purchase rate in a way that attributes or compensates the originating partner."

## Solution Signals

- **Commission attribution for online sales originating from spa consultations** — implied by the partner's grievance. If the consulting partner received credit for the online sale, the channel conflict would resolve.
- **Digital Beauty Pass** — implied by the paper limitation. Spa analysis data could travel digitally to the online shop, creating both a client bridge and an attribution mechanism.
